Winter War

Political Change

The outcome of the war and the surprising victory of Finland directly affected decisions made by the German and Soviet armies throughout WW2. This is because once Germany saw that a small and weak country like Finland could win a war against the Soviet Union, they underestimated the true power of the Soviet army. Not only this, but the confilct also showcased the lack of power and authority of the League Of Nations, who were supposed to prevent war between their members but failed to intervene when the USSR tried to invade Finland.
