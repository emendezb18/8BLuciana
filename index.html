Although it is not as known as other wars like WW2 or the Vietnam War, it still holds and extremely significant role because of the effects it had on WW2 and the Soviet Union's downfall. Before the Winter War

Finland: It was an independent country with internationally recognized borders.
Soviet Union: It had borders that extended to the regions near Finland.

After the Winter War

Finland: Despite having given up some areas, it maintained its independence and new borders were established with the Soviet Union. Finland ceded parts of Carelia and other areas to the west, as well as the Hanko Peninsula, which was leased to the Soviet Union.
Soviet Union: Soviet Union: Managed to expand its territory at the expense of Finland, a cquiring important territories in Karelia and other areas in western Finland.

In conclusion the Winter War had a significant impact on the political boundaries between Finland and the Soviet Union, with the Soviet Union gaining territory at Finland's expense as a result of territorial cessions imposed at the end of the conflict.

A brief history of the participating countries 15 years after the war.

Finland began post-war reconstruction, maintaining its independence but had to cede part of its territory to the Soviet Union and it was not until 1950 (10 years later) that the reconstruction of its economy and infrastructure began. the Soviet Union began post-war reconstruction under the leadership of Joseph Stalin until his death in 1953. After Stalin's death, there was a power struggle and eventually Nikita Khrushchev came to power, which caused many significant changes, some good and some bad.
